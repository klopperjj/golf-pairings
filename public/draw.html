Day 1 · Thursday
Scramble Drive
Par 4s & Par 5s
Both partners tee off. The best drive is selected (no limit on how many times each partner's drive may be used) and both play their own ball from that spot. Par 3s play normally — each player plays their own tee shot. From the second shot onwards each player plays their own ball.
Day 2 · Friday
Normal Play
All Holes
Each player plays their own ball throughout. Standard rules of golf apply (with the local rules below).
Three Competitions Running In Parallel
🏆 The Cup · Team A Holes vs Team Bum Bandits
Four-Ball Better-Ball Stableford — the better of the two partners' Stableford points counts for the team on each hole. Three fourballs per day × 2 days. Cumulative across both days — the team with the most points after 36 holes wins the cup. Played on both days.
👥 Best Pair · Day 1 + Best Pair · Day 2 (separate prizes)
Aggregate Stableford — both partners' individual Stableford points are added together for the pair (not better-ball). All 6 pairs ranked head-to-head each day. Day 1 and Day 2 are independent — there's a Best Pair prize for each day.
🏌️ Best Individual · Day 2 only
Individual Stableford — each player's own Stableford score on Day 2 (Normal Play). Top individual gets a separate prize. Not contested on Day 1 because Scramble Drive shares tee shots so individual scores aren't fully independent.
Handicap allowance
All competitions use 85% of course handicap. Strokes received per stroke index. Live leaderboard and score-entry app handle the math automatically.

Scramble Drive · Day 1 · Par 4s & Par 5s
The tee shot
Both partners tee off on par 4s and par 5s. The best drive is selected by the partnership — there is no limit on how many drives of each partner may be used during the round. The ball is marked and both players place their ball within one club length of that spot — no closer to the hole, same condition (rough stays rough).
Par 3s — normal play
Scramble Drive does not apply on par 3s. Each player plays their own tee shot and their own ball for the entire hole.
From the second shot onwards
Each player plays their own ball for the remainder of the hole. Normal rules apply. The better net score of the two partners counts for the hole.
Stableford scoring
Each hole scores Stableford points based on net score (better-ball: the higher of the two partners' Stableford points counts for the team). The team with the most cumulative points across 18 holes wins the round.
Selecting the drive
Both partners drive, then the partnership selects the best ball before lifting either ball. The chosen drive is marked and both players drop within one club length — no closer to the hole, same condition. Once the drive is selected there is no switching — if it lands in the rough, both play from the rough. If both drives are lost or OB, both players re-tee under penalty.
Local Rules · Both Days
Placing
Fairway only. Place within one club length of the ball's position — no closer to the hole. Rough, bunkers, and fringe play as they lie. No placing in hazards.
Gimmes
Within the leather of the putter. Must be conceded by the opponent — you cannot take your own gimme. If not conceded, the putt must be holed out.

Live Scoring App · How It Works
Where to find it
golf-pairings.vercel.app — open it in Safari on iPhone, Chrome on Android, or any browser on your laptop. Live leaderboard works for everyone (wives, kids, the 19th hole) without signing in.
Add to home screen — iPhone
Open the link in Safari (not Chrome — must be Safari for the home-screen icon to work). Tap Share (square with arrow) → "Add to Home Screen" → name it SFGC Invitational → Add. The green flag icon launches it like a real app — no Safari toolbar.
Add to home screen — Android
Open in Chrome → tap the ⋮ menu top right → "Add to Home screen" → Add.
Signing in
Tap Score at the bottom. Sign in with your mobile number + the 4-digit PIN Juan WhatsApps you on tournament morning. Mobile in 0821234567 format. Session lasts 48 hours so you only sign in once for the weekend. Locked out → message Juan.
Entering Scores · Anyone in the Fourball
Shared entry
Any one of the four players in your fourball can enter scores for all 4 players — whoever has signal and their phone out. No need to log in as each individual player.
Hole-by-hole
Each hole defaults to par. Tap + / − to adjust each player's gross score. Stableford points and birdie/par/bogey labels update live. Hit Save Hole to lock it in and advance to the next hole. Hit Reset if you need to clear all 4 players for the current hole.
Cumulative scoreboard
A running better-ball Stableford total for your matchup is shown at the top of the score page so you always know how the fourball stands.
End-of-round certification
Once all 18 holes are entered, the certification panel unlocks at the bottom of the score page. One player from each side (one A Hole + one Bum Bandit) taps "Approve Full Scorecard" to finalise. One certification covers all 18 holes — replaces signing the paper card. Until then, scores stay editable.
Live Leaderboard · What You'll See
The Match · overall team total (cup decider)
A Holes vs Bum Bandits — Four-Ball Better-Ball Stableford (best of the pair each hole counts for the team) accumulated across both days combined. The total at the top of the leaderboard is the cup. Per-day team head-to-head is also shown inside each Pairs tab.
Pairs · Day 1 and Day 2 (separate prizes)
All 6 pairs ranked by Aggregate Stableford for the day — meaning both partners' Stableford points are added together (not better-ball). Day 1 and Day 2 are scored independently with their own prizes for best pair.
Individuals · Day 2 only
Day 2 has a third leaderboard tracking each player's individual Stableford points. Since everyone plays their own ball off the tee on Friday, individual scoring is meaningful. Top individual gets a separate prize.
Live updates
The leaderboard updates in real time the moment any score is saved — no refresh needed. The "Live · Updated HH:MM:SS" tag at the bottom confirms data is fresh.
