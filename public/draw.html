Day 1 · Thursday
Scramble Drive
Four-Ball Better Ball Match Play
Both partners tee off. The best drive is selected — both players then play their own ball from that spot for the remainder of the hole. The better net score of the pair contests each hole. Holes are won, halved, or lost. Strokes received per stroke index at 85% of course handicap.
Day 2 · Friday
Normal Play
Four-Ball Better Ball Match Play
Each player plays their own ball throughout. The better net score of the two partners contests each hole against the opposing pair. Holes are won, halved, or lost. Strokes received per stroke index at 85% of course handicap.

SFGC Tour · Unofficial & Slightly Dangerous
The A Holes
JK
Juan Klopper
The Wildcard
Holds the highest handicap in the group — and earns every stroke of it. Makes a 3-wood off the deck look like a 300-yard EA Sports highlight. Somehow cannot replicate this off the tee box. At all. Can card a brilliant round or completely unravel before the turn. Beware. On any given day, either the story of the weekend or the reason someone loses a bet. Nobody knows which Juan is showing up. Including Juan.
JL
James Leach
The Purist
A 2 handicap with 2-handicap opinions about everything. The most vocal golf theologian in the group — has a view on every ruling, every pin position, and every perceived breach of etiquette. Despises LIV Golf with the energy of a man personally wronged by it. If you mention Saudi money near the first tee, bring a hard hat.
DH
David "Hollywood Harry" Harrison
The Window Finder
An 11 handicap with an almost supernatural ability to escape trouble. Has never found a ball behind a tree that couldn't find a window. The shot probably shouldn't come off. It usually does. Wildcard energy — could play a brilliant back nine or manufacture the most creative route around the course you have ever witnessed.
RoA
Ross Andrews
The Silent Storm
Four kids. This tour is what keeps him sane. Kenyan, with a swing that belongs on an 18-year-old Tiger Woods highlight reel — smooth, effortless, and deeply unfair. The calmest demeanour in the group right up until he gets on a run. Then watch out. He is scoring low and saying nothing about it.
BR
Byron Roos
The Commissioner
A 20 handicap running the show with the authority of a scratch golfer. Founder, organiser, judge, jury, and executioner. Removed someone from the Tour group mid-conversation. Claims it was necessary. The evicted party may disagree. Runs the chat like a benevolent dictatorship. Voting is a formality. Byron already knows the outcome.
JP
Jean-Pierre Du Toit
The Quiet Assassin
Playing off 18, so the strokes are generous and the damage is real. Too busy for the WhatsApp group — archived the chat before it had finished loading. Secretly a LIV sympathiser — won't start the conversation, but won't end it either. Suddenly winning on Sunday and nobody knows how. Brought his brother Mike along, which is either family loyalty or a very deliberate strategic move.

SFGC Tour · Unofficial & Slightly Dangerous
The Bum Bandits
RA
Rob Arnold
The People's Champion
The sharpest commentator in the group and universally adored. Off a 5, good enough to back it up. Actually attended the SA LIV tournament — filed no complaints. Has a gift for saying exactly the right thing at exactly the right moment. The tour runs better when Rob is in a good mood, and Rob is almost always in a good mood.
ND
Nic Dunn
The Mayor
The friendliest person on the tour by a considerable margin. An 11 handicap who plays it straight — on the course and off it. So committed to the weekend he booked his own second villa. Nobody asked. Nobody had to. That's just Nic. The social glue that stops the whole thing from becoming a logistics disaster.
CG
Charles Garner
The Camo Cowboy
Deep South American, transplanted to SA 12 years ago after falling for a South African girl — best decision he ever made, second only to the camo. Grand Pappy RIP passed down the love of LIV, the Yeehaw energy, and the lifestyle. Loud about all of it. Not remotely sorry. Renowned for excavating divots of extraordinary size and depth — the kind that make greenkeepers weep and agronomists file incident reports. Knows exactly how to drive a cart and have a good time. Dangerous in a scramble. Yeehaw.
SM
Shaheed Mohamed
The Fund Manager
Fifteen years at Allan Gray, where he managed everyone's hard-earned investments — and spent a good portion of that time trying to get you into the Multi Manager Fund. Now striking out on his own. The pitch may have changed, but the composure hasn't. A 20 handicap with the calm of a man who has watched markets crash and recovered without blinking. Treat any investment conversation like a par 5 — take your time, don't rush, and read the fine print.
JA
Jason Airey
The Competitor
Off a 9 and not afraid to use it. Recently welcomed a new baby — life is easy for Jason. Presumably less so for his wife. Close friends with Jean-Pierre and Charles, which means he arrived informed, motivated, and possibly running on very little sleep. Super competitive — tracks every stroke, every Nassau, every edge. The friendliness is real, but so is the scorecard. Don't mistake the smile for softness.
MD
Mike Du Toit
The Hazard
Jean-Pierre's brother, off 11. On the course: a classified hazard — unpredictable, unavoidable, and somehow always in your line. The kind of player whose ball finds the most inconvenient spot on the hole. Off the course, presumably delightful. The Du Toit package deal. The tour accepts the terms.
